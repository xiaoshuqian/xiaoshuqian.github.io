Emotional Health

  • Deal with unfairness
    • Science:
      • Human brain is wired to react to unfairness with indignation, resulting in impaired judgement (logical thinking). So don't beat yourself for feeling angry. After all, this is a natural response. Focus on a wise response.
      • Everyone plays by their own rules and believes their own rules are the fair ones. These rules are formed by one's own up-bringing and are very hard if not impossible for others to change.
    • Catch yourself when detecting unfairness and stop reacting right away. Momentarily exitricate yourself from the situation if possible. Otherwise try to take a deep breath and free your thoughts from the situation even for just a moment
    • Assess the situation: Did you really lose anything? What's the impact? If not, try to focus on something more positive and productive. Otherwise, try to formulate a strategy to best address the situation
    • Sometimes, better choosing healthy than right
  • The science of well-being
    • Weekly rewirement:
      • Week 1. Use your character strengths
        • Identify character strengths. Mine: love of learning, love, prudence and curiosity
          • You can measure your PERMA score here. PERMA = Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationship, Meaning, Accomplishment, Health
        • Use and strengthen your strengths. See tips here and here
      • Week 2. Savoring and gratitude journaling
      • Week 3. Acts of kindness and social connection
      • Week 4. Exercise and sleep
      • Week 5. Meditation and gratitude letter/visit
      • Week 6. Time affluence for doing what you want: using your strengths, social connection, meditation, exercise, sleep, etc
      • Week 7-10. Final rewirement
        • Which rewirement did you pick and why?
        • Did you socially committ to the rewirement?
        • Did you utilize situation support or goal setting strategies?
        • How many times you did you PLAN to do the rewirement and how many times did you ACTUALLY do it? How did you track your progress?
        • Did your happiness score change over time?
        • How was the overall experience for you?
    • Week1: Using your character strengths
      • Here character strength is defined as strengths that are fullfiling and intrinsically valuable (see wiki). Thus, using these strengths improve your well-being.
      • Practicing the following strengths and using their associative catch-phrases can be effective (see Strength mantra improves your day on VIA):
        • Gratitude: Thanks-you, thank-you!
        • Forgiveness: It's OK, it's OK!
        • Curiosity: Cool, cool! Interesting, interesting!
    • Week 2: Act to counter misconceptions about happiness
      • Knowing is not nearly half the battle. Act: write gratitude journal, practice savoring, applying your strength
      • Our minds' strongest intuitions are sometimes wrong and knowing it may not change your intuition. e.g. Ponzo illusion
      • People are very adaptive: predicted happyness and sadness are often proved to be exagerated. Act: Enjoy the process and do not get overly fixated on the result
        • People will often demand higher salary regardless of their current salary.
        • People will often attain good job/money/body and other fancy stuffs without getting happier.
      • Factors that determine your happieness: 50% genetic set point, 10% life circumstance, 40% your intentional actions and thoughts (cf Sonja Lyubomirsky)
    • Week 3: Annoying features of the mind
      • Our minds don't think in terms of absolute but judge to relative ref points
      • Our intuitions are often wrong
      • Our minds are built to get used to things
      • We often don't realize that our minds are built to get used to things
    • Week 4: Overcome bias (or annoying features of the mind)
      • Thwart hedonic adaptation
      • Rethink 'awesome stuff'
      • Reset your ref points
    • Week 5: Better wantings (instead of good job/money/luxuary, etc):
      • Kindness and social connection
      • Time affluence
      • Mind control
        • A wondering mind is an unhappy one. Science: Default Mode Network
        • Meditation: control mind wondering, improve focus, strengthen social connection, improve grade
      • Healthy practice: exercise and sleep
    • Week 6: Put your wantings into practice
      • Situation support: Make it easier to do healthy things
        • Wansink et al. (2006). The office candy dish: proximityâ€™s influence on estimated and actual consumption.
          • Put chocolate on the desk vs in the drawer (visual clue)
          • Put chocolate on the desk vs one meter away (convenience)
        • Wansink et al. (2016). Slim by design: kitchen counter correlates of obesity
        • Do things with friends
      • Goal setting: SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time bounded)
        • Make specific and measurable: 5W
      • Implementation planning (MCII, WOOP)
        • MCII: Mental Contrasting with Implementation Intention
          • MC: Visualize the outcomes/benefits that can be reaped from achieving the goals, followed by possible obstacles
          • II: an if-then plan
        • WOOP: Wish, Outcomes, Obstacles and (an implementation or if-then) Plan
          • Gabriele Oettingen, Rethinking Positive Thinking: Inside the New Science of Motivation (a nice introduction to the work on using implementation intention and WOOP in your daily life)
